4th Axis Positioning help

Four and five axis mills can create complicated geometries, but in many applications, the rotary is used only for positioning the part. In these applications, it's not necessary to rely on CAD/CAM every step of the way. As long as know know where the axis is located and where your point is relative to the axis, calculating a rotational transformation is trivial.

First define the axis of rotation, either A or B. This choice determines which plane is observed in the graph. On a vertical machine we'll be looking at the YZ plane, but on a horizontal machine we'll be looking at the XZ plane. Next, locate the axis by defining a vector starting at machine zero, ending on the axis of rotation. Then, define a point measured from the center of rotation.

The calculated result is the location of the point relative to machine zero, after rotation is applied.
